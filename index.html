Operational Philosophy

I build environments out of materials most people throw away. I integrate fluorescent pigments, UV blacklights, and custom fluid dynamics into salvaged industrial hardware. I treat my neighborhood like a proving ground. Every build is a first—first kinetic fountain, first paving job, first hand-poured stone arch.

I don't buy materials. I find them. And I don't walk away from an unfinished job.

Project 01 // Kinetic Art

3-Zone Kinetic Water Architecture

A massive, closed-loop kinetic water feature utilizing dual-spectrum municipal-grade UV tracer dyes (Green Fluorescein & Red Rhodamine) and phosphorescent Strontium Aluminate. Features a 4.5-foot suspended hydraulic hammer, a 12-foot wind-driven steel gyroscope with weeping lines, and a cascading bucket relay.

POWERED BY A UNIFIED 41-GALLON GRAVITY-EQUALIZED FLUID ENGINE.

Project 02 // Structural

Front Yard Proving Ground

Complete property hardscape. Built a hand-dug adobe pond, welded snake head sculpture, hand-poured stone arch, UV-painted flagstone mosaic paths, hand-formed faux boulders, and raised garden beds from salvaged fence lumber. Built in under 3 months, solo.

Project 03 // Commercial

Coastal Barn Build

Custom barn build near the coast by the Guard station. Managed a crew of six for full interior and exterior finishing, including structural foundations, electrical runs, and a massive middle rock staging area. One of our largest structural builds.

Project 04 // Community

Backyard Transformations & Retaining Walls

Complete patio overhauls, paver installations, lattice and fence work, and heavy-duty retaining walls designed specifically for elderly neighbors needing accessible, usable, and safe outdoor space.

The Builder
